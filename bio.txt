Shashank is currently a Ph.D. candidate in the department of Electrical engineering and Computer science (EECS) at MIT. His research interests are at the intersection of machine learning, program analysis, and cognitive neuroscience. His research has been published at highly selective global venues like ICLR and KDD, and he holds three patents. Prior to his Ph.D. studies, he was a senior research engineer at Aspiring Minds' research lab, where he helped build, deploy, and manage a number of innovative products involving machine learning. These products are used by >1M job applicants across the world today. He is also broadly interested in governance, science education, and policy. To understand the nuances of how state governments deliver benefits to the last-mile, he has spent a year in rural Bihar in India.
